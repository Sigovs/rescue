# Sports Car Rescue — Story Flow

The page is built as one narrative, not a stack of sections. It follows a seller
from the moment *before they let something go* → to *reaching out*. Read top to
bottom, it's a conversation with a passionate collector — **"more collector than
dealer."**

> Emotional spine: *"If I have a collector car, project, barn find, or a box of
> vintage parts — this is the person I should call before I sell or throw
> anything away."*

---

## The arc, section by section

1. **Hero — the hook / first contact**
   *"Before you sell it, scrap it, or clean it out — Let's talk."*
   Catches the seller at the decision moment. Empathy + invitation, not a pitch.
   Collage of polaroids, title document, speedo, parts tag flies in on load.

2. **What We Buy — "We buy stories, not just cars."**
   *What* we're interested in, by condition/feeling: Barn Finds · Unfinished
   Projects · Estate Collections · Parts & Memorabilia. Signals we value history,
   not just metal. (Teaser, not the full catalogue.)

3. **Cars For Sale — "From the collection."** *(inventory carousel)*
   Proof we're real and active — a rotating showroom of rescued, sorted cars
   with mileage + price ("Call for price" / "$…"). Builds credibility and desire.
   The one overtly "sales" beat; everything else stays collector-voiced.

4. **Why Sports Car Rescue — "Most people see clutter. We see history."** *(dark)*
   The manifesto / who we are: built by an enthusiast and collector, **not a
   dealership**. + how we work — Private Buyer · Fast Evaluation · Collector
   Knowledge. This is the **About Us** beat. Earns trust.

5. **Cars We Buy — "Cars we buy."** *(model grid)*
   The specifics: the makes & models we actively hunt (American muscle + select
   imports), each with a note. Turns the feeling into a concrete "do you have one?"

6. **Estate & Collection Assistance — "Inherited a collection? Don't clear it out yet."**
   Care for families/executors at a hard moment. "Don't throw this away" field
   note + keys + an old plate. A unique, lower-pressure lead channel.

7. **Contact — "Found something worth saving? Let's talk."** *(dark, the close)*
   The payoff. Hook + direct ways to reach us + a light-paper intake form
   ("Tell us what you found"). Every CTA on the page leads here.

8. **Cars We Purchased — Instagram feed** *(above footer)*
   Social proof / track record. Real rescues, draggable strip, IG-branded.
   Reassures the hesitant seller right before the footer.

9. **Footer** — practical close: pages, address, socials, heavy dark base.

---

## Design rhythm (supports the story)

- **Light ↔ dark cadence** paces the read like chapters:
  Hero (paper) → What We Buy (paper) → For Sale (kraft) → **Why (dark)** →
  Cars We Buy (paper) → Estate (kraft) → **Contact (dark)** → IG/footer (dark base).
- **Archival voice throughout** — polaroids, taped notes, typewriter labels,
  handwriting, kraft paper, plates, keys. Never SaaS/dealership.
- **Rust = action** (CTAs, hover bars, price). **Brass = the collector's gold.**
- One **script line per beat** ("Let's talk.", "every car has a story.") keeps a
  human, handwritten thread running through it.

---

## How to pick this up later

- Keep the spine: *empathy → we value it → we're real → we're trustworthy →
  here's what we want → we help even in hard moments → reach out → proof.*
- New sections should slot into that arc, not just append.
- Outstanding real-data swaps live in memory: Formspree, email, IG, address,
  inventory listings (all `data-placeholder` in markup).
